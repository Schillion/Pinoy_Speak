    \begin{abstractwithpageno}
      \\
      \textbf{\AUTHOR}, University of the Philippines Los Ba\~{n}os,
      \MONTH\ \YEAR. \textbf{\TITLE}\\
      Adviser: CONCEPCION L. KHAN\\

      Informal Filipino and Taglish evolve rapidly in online
      spaces, making them difficult to process using static
      dictionaries and conventional Natural Language Processing
      (NLP) resources. This study presents \textit{Pinoy Speak},
      a deployed full-stack system for detecting, organizing, and
      explaining emerging Filipino internet slang. The system
      collects public text from selected Filipino Reddit
      communities, YouTube comments, and user chat interactions.
      As of the current implementation, it has archived 175,481
      records and maintains approximately 104 verified slang
      entries. Candidate terms are processed through a pipeline
      that combines calamanCy tokenization, subword novelty
      signals, FastText nearest-neighbor evidence, temporal
      burstiness analysis, morphological filtering, and
      context-window disambiguation for ambiguous slang words.
      Verified candidates are enriched through a separate LLM
      pipeline using Google Gemini, Groq Llama, and Ollama, while
      the user-facing ``Kuya Slang'' tutor uses
      retrieval-augmented access to the live lexicon. Evaluation
      consisted of a 79-entry lexicon validation with three
      ratings per entry and a 36-response consented beta
      usability survey. Lexicon validation produced high
      definition correctness ratings (mean = 4.83/5) and example
      naturalness ratings (mean = 4.75/5), while beta usability
      results showed strong user acceptance across the dictionary,
      tutor, analyzer, navigation, and trend modules.

      \vspace{1em}
      \noindent\textit{Keywords:} Informal Filipino, Taglish,
      slang detection, lexical trends, natural language
      processing, FastText, calamanCy, retrieval-augmented
      generation.

    \end{abstractwithpageno}
